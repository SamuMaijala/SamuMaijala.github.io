Terminology

  • Database: A place to store and organize information so it can be easily found and used.
  • Cloud Computing: Using the internet to store and access files or run programs instead of your computer.
  • Cybersecurity: Keeping computers, programs, and data safe from hackers.
  • Data Analytics: Looking at data to find useful information or patterns.
  • Software Development: The process of making apps, programs, or websites.
  • ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning): A program that helps businesses manage things like money, people, and products in one system.
  • CRM (Customer Relationship Management): A tool to keep track of customers and make sure they’re happy.
  • Agile: A way to plan and build things step by step, so changes are easier to make.
  • Big Data: Really large sets of information that need special tools to understand.
  • UX (User Experience): How easy and enjoyable it is to use a program, app, or website.

Pathway into BIT

  • Step 1 - Understand BIT Basics: Learn what Business Information Technology (BIT) is and why it’s important. Start with beginner-friendly resources like articles or videos.
  • Step 2 - Identify Your Goals: Decide whether you’re interested in programming, data analytics, IT management, or another BIT-related area.
  • Step 3 - Learn Foundational Skills: Study basic programming languages like Python, HTML, and SQL, as well as essential IT concepts.
  • Step 4 - Explore Tools: Familiarize yourself with tools like Microsoft Office, databases, project management apps, and collaboration platforms.
  • Step 5 - Join Communities: Participate in forums like Stack Overflow, Reddit (e.g., r/learnprogramming), or local meetups to connect with others in the field.
  • Step 6 - Practice with Projects: Apply your knowledge to hands-on projects like building a website, creating a database, or analyzing data.
  • Step 7 - Take Online Courses: Enroll in courses on platforms like Coursera, Udemy, or freeCodeCamp to deepen your knowledge in specific areas.
  • Step 8 - Build a Portfolio: Showcase your skills and projects in a professional portfolio to demonstrate your abilities to employers.
  • Step 9 - Gain Certifications: Obtain certifications in relevant areas, such as CompTIA, AWS, or Microsoft certifications.
  • Step 10 - Start Applying: Look for internships, entry-level jobs, or freelance opportunities in BIT to gain real-world experience.
